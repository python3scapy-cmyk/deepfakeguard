1. Controller Identity

This session is operated by the DeepfakeGuard Team. For any questions about this notice or your data, contact: placeholder@example.com [fill in real contact email before any real deployment].

2. Categories of Data

  • Face embedding — a mathematical vector representation of facial features, not a photograph.
  • Voice embedding — a mathematical vector representation of vocal characteristics, not an audio recording.
  • Session metadata — timestamps and liveness-check flags associated with the verification session.
We do not collect photographs, audio recordings, or video files.

3. Legal Basis

  • GDPR: Explicit consent under Article 9(2)(a) for processing special category (biometric) data.
  • US / BIPA: Written consent obtained via the in-app consent modal before any data collection begins.

4. Retention & Deletion

All data is retained for the duration of the verification session only. When the session ends — whether by closing the browser tab, closing the app, or clicking "End Session" — the face and voice embeddings are dereferenced and garbage-collected from memory. No data is written to disk at any point.

5. Third-Party Disclosure

None. All processing occurs on your device. No data is transmitted to our servers or any third party.

This statement reflects the architecture confirmed by the engineering team as of this document's last update (see consent-copy-v1.md in the repository for the underlying confirmation). If the system architecture changes — for example, if any model is moved to a cloud-hosted endpoint — this section must be revised before that change ships.

6. Your Rights

  • Right to withdraw consent — you may end the session at any time, which immediately stops all data collection.
  • Right to erasure — session data is automatically deleted upon ending the session; no further action is required on your part.

7. Data Security

Embeddings are held in RAM only. They are never persisted to localStorage, IndexedDB, or the filesystem. Communication between the verification modules occurs entirely in-memory within the same session.
