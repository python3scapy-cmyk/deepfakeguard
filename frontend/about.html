What we do

Verifying that the person on the call is a person — and the right one

DeepfakeGuard is a real-time, multi-modal verification system. It checks a live video call for synthetic faces, replayed or injected video, cloned voices, and identity swaps mid-session — and turns those checks into one trust score a human can act on.

In 2024 an employee at the engineering firm Arup transferred roughly $25M after a video call in which every other participant was a deepfake. The call looked normal. That is the gap this system is built for: the moment a face on a screen stops being evidence of a person.

How the verification works

A verifier opens a session and shares a six-character code. The person being verified joins with that code in a browser — no install, no app. Their camera and microphone stream is analysed continuously, and five independent signals are fused into a single trust score, with hard-deny rules that override the score when a check fails outright.

Visual deepfake A transformer image classifier scores each sampled face crop for generative artefacts, smoothed over a rolling window so one noisy frame can't swing a verdict.
Liveness Randomised challenges the user must perform on cue — blink twice, turn your head left or right — measured from a 468-point face mesh. A photo, a looping clip and a pre-rendered deepfake all fail this, because none of them can respond to a prompt issued a second ago.
Voice A spoken-phrase challenge: the system picks a random phrase, the user says it, and the same clip is checked twice — speech recognition confirms the phrase was actually spoken, and an anti-spoofing model trained on ASVspoof 2019 checks the voice is not synthetic or replayed.
Identity continuity A face embedding taken at the start of the session is re-checked throughout it, so swapping the person — or substituting a video — part-way through is caught.
Injection & replay Virtual-camera and frame-timing checks for streams fed in from software rather than a real capture device.

What we offer

Live session verification

Browser-based, no install for the person being verified. Multiple clients can join one verifier's session; each gets an independent trust score, challenge history and audit trail.

Media forensics on upload

Drop in a photo, video or audio file and get the same pipeline applied offline — deepfake probability, spoof-frame ratio, identity drift across frames, and audio spoof scoring.

Verifier console

A PIN-gated dashboard: live trust score with its band, per-signal breakdown, the reason a score moved, and a timestamped log of every challenge and verdict.

Explainable verdicts

Every score comes with the signal that dragged it down and the rule that fired. A verifier is never asked to trust a number they can't interrogate.

Design principles

No silent degradation. If a model fails to load, the system says so and abstains rather than emitting a confident-looking number from a mock.

Absent is not neutral. A signal that never arrived is excluded from the fusion rather than scored as "average", so a missing check can't quietly manufacture a passing grade.

Privacy by default. Frames are analysed in memory and discarded. Consent is collected before the camera opens, and what is collected, why, and how long it is kept is stated in plain language on the consent screen.
